Week 4: Laser Cutting & Engraving


Using MakerCase, I created a box and added designs onto each of the faces. Using the laser cutter, I cut out the box on cardboard with vector engraving. Each face of the box had cut outs of different human silouhettes. With Coreldraw, I designed a press-fit kit with the letters J A N E. I had to measure the thickness of the cardboard to create cutouts on the letters that would allow the pieces to fit together snugly. Also using Coreldraw, I created a design for my cork coaster. I placed a photo of a BTS album cover onto a coaster template, then I found the band members' signatures online and pasted them onto the template so it would look like a signed album.


For my sublimation printing project, I took two photos and pasted them onto a template for a mug in CorelDraw. I then flipped and printed the design, cut it out, taped it onto my mug, and used a heat press to transfer the design onto the mug. After removing the mug from the heat press, I had to submerge it in water to cool it down.


Both of these processes were super interesting and it was exciting being able to create customized products. I was very satisfied with how my creations turned out, and I would definitely use these machines again in the future once I come up with more design ideas.
