Week 4: Garment Design & Sublimation Printing


Using Photoshop, I created a magazine cover design for my shirt. I found a photo of a celebrity, cut him out, and placed it next to a photo of me. I refined the edges and tones so the manipulation would look more natural, then found a magazine cover and replaced the cover photo with my newly created image. Once I finished my design, I headed over to the Brother printer to print it onto my shirt. I steam-pressed the shirt beforehand to remove wrinkles, and again after printing to ensure that the ink would set.


For my sublimation printing project, I took two photos and pasted them onto a template for a mug in CorelDraw. I then flipped and printed the design, cut it out, taped it onto my mug, and used a heat press to transfer the design onto the mug. After removing the mug from the heat press, I had to submerge it in water to cool it down.


Both of these processes were super interesting and it was exciting being able to create customized products. I was very satisfied with how my creations turned out, and I would definitely use these machines again in the future once I come up with more design ideas.
